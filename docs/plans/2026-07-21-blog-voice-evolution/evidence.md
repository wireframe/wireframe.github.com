## Primals anchor (Feb 2023, measured)

Source: Roam block `((NKUuTwhnE))`, page `[[Primal world beliefs]]`. Self-assessment done during a BetterUp Executive Leadership Team intensive (Miami). "Primals" = deeply-held, mostly-subconscious, stable beliefs about what the world is fundamentally like (Jer Clifton / Marty Seligman research). Scale ~1–5, midpoint 3.0.

| Dimension | Score | Plain-English meaning |
|---|---|---|
| Good (overarching) | 3.52 | The world is basically good; ambiguity probably hides good things |
| Enticing | 3.82 | The world is interesting, beautiful, worth exploring |
| Alive | 3.79 | The world is responsive and meaningful, not cold/mechanical |
| Safe | 3.28 | Leans safe, but the most guarded of the four |

Profile: optimist-explorer, moderate (not naive) sense of safety. This is the **only measured world-view datapoint**; all per-era world-view reads elsewhere are inference from the text.

## Fixed→growth reversal pairs (the headline evidence)

1. **Talent: innate-and-sortable → universal-and-conditional**
   - Before (2005): "people that just don't \"get it\"" — `_posts/2005-10-10-real-world.md`
   - After (2026): "Talent is equally distributed. Opportunity is not." — `_posts/2026-05-30-engineering-your-remote-engineering-team.md`

2. **The user: blame → empathy**
   - Before: "I was absolutely confident [it] was \"User Error\" (PEBCAK was my term of choice to talk down on others who didn't understand the elegance of systems I was building)." — `_posts/2023-11-23-my-no-shame-november-story.md`
   - After (same post, the turn): reframes that habit as shame and pivots to customer responsiveness — `_posts/2023-11-23-my-no-shame-november-story.md`

3. **Nuance (candor):** growth-minded about *skills/technology* in every era (relentless self-teaching even in Era 1); the *fixed* mindset lived only in the social/self-evaluative layer — judging others' potential and tying self-worth to being right.
