    Where it comes from

    Cynicism grows from a lack of belief and trust. A cynic is an idealist whose ideals hardened into expectations of other people, then went unmet.

    Senge, The Fifth Discipline

    Why it’s tempting

    “Cynicism is cowardice. It takes courage to care.” Resignation is the self-soothing posture that avoids the vulnerability of hope.

    Gen. James Mattis, + your collected notes

    The one move underneath it all

    Frustrated idealist → Faith-grounded Stoic

    The early self converted high ideals about craft into expectations of everyone around him, and turned contemptuous when they went unmet (“drones,” “people who don’t get it”). The current self practices the antidote he later wrote down: stay clear and kind, mean what you say, choose hope. That is the same discipline Stoicism asks for and the same one a deepening faith rests on — hold the ideal, and drop the demand that the world comply with it.
